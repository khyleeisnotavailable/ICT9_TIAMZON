Tree

By Joyce Kilmer

I think
that I shall
never see a poem
lovely as a tree a tree
A tree whose hungry mouth
is prest againts the earth's sweet
flowing breast; a tree that looks at God
all day and lifts her leafy arms to pray;
a tree that may in Summer Wear
a nest of robins in her hair;
Upon whose bosom snow
has lain; who intimately
lives with rain

Poems are made
by fools like
me but only
God can make
a tree

Khylee Tiamzon
